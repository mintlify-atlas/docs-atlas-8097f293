# Quick Start Guide

This guide will help you get up and running with Namida in just a few minutes. Let's start playing your favorite music!

## First Launch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Namida">
    Launch Namida for the first time. You'll see an onboarding screen with configuration options.
    
    <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/namidaco/namida/main/screens/customization_settings.jpg" alt="Namida Settings" width="300" />
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Grant Storage Permission">
    Namida needs access to your device storage to scan and play your music files.
    
    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Android">
        When prompted, tap **Allow** to grant storage permissions:
        - **Files and media** (Android 13+)
        - **Storage** (Android 12 and below)
        
        <Note>
        For advanced features like tag editing and downloads, you may need to grant "All files access" later.
        </Note>
      </Tab>
      
      <Tab title="Windows/Linux">
        You'll configure your music folders in the next step. No system permissions needed.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Music Folders">
    Tell Namida where your music files are located:
    
    1. Go to **Settings** → **Indexer**
    2. Tap **Folders to Scan**
    3. Add folders containing your music:
       - Android: Usually `/storage/emulated/0/Music` or `/sdcard/Music`
       - Windows: Usually `C:\Users\YourName\Music`
       - Linux: Usually `~/Music` or `/home/username/Music`
    
    <Info>
    Namida automatically suggests common music folder locations. By default, it includes the `Music` and `Download` folders.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wait for Indexing">
    Namida will scan your folders and index all music files. This may take a few moments depending on your library size.
    
    <Note>
    The indexer reads metadata from your audio files (title, artist, album, artwork, etc.) and organizes them in the library.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Playing Your First Track

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a Song">
    Navigate to **Tracks** view to see all your music. You can:
    - Scroll through the list
    - Use the search icon 🔍 at the top
    - Sort by tapping the sort button
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap to Play">
    Simply tap any track to start playing. The miniplayer will appear at the bottom.
    
    <Info>
    Long-press a track to see more options like adding to queue, playlist, or viewing info.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use the Player">
    Swipe up on the miniplayer to expand it to full screen. Here you can:
    - See album artwork
    - View lyrics (if available)
    - Adjust equalizer settings
    - Control playback speed and pitch
    - See waveform visualization
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Essential Player Controls

### Miniplayer Controls

The bottom miniplayer shows:
- **Play/Pause** button
- **Previous/Next** track buttons
- **Track progress** bar (tap to seek)
- **Album artwork** (tap to expand player)
- **Queue icon** (see what's playing next)

### Full Player Gestures

<Info>
Namida supports intuitive gestures for quick control:
</Info>

- **Swipe down**: Minimize player
- **Swipe left/right**: Skip to next/previous track
- **Double-tap artwork**: Toggle lyrics view
- **Long-press artwork**: Custom action (configurable)
- **Tap progress bar**: Seek to position

## Creating Your First Playlist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Tracks">
    Long-press on a track to enter selection mode, then tap other tracks to select multiple songs.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add to Playlist">
    Tap the **+** icon at the top, then select **Add to Playlist** → **Create New Playlist**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Name Your Playlist">
    Enter a name for your playlist and tap **Create**.
    
    <Note>
    You can set custom artwork for playlists by opening the playlist and tapping the artwork.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exploring YouTube Features

One of Namida's standout features is seamless YouTube integration.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access YouTube Tab">
    Open the navigation drawer and tap **YouTube** to switch to YouTube mode.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Search for Music">
    Tap the search icon and enter a song name, artist, or video title.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Stream or Download">
    - **Tap** a video to stream it instantly
    - **Long-press** to see download options
    - Choose audio-only or video+audio format
    
    <Info>
    Downloaded content is cached locally for offline playback!
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Manage Cache">
    Go to **Settings** → **YouTube** → **Cache** to manage downloaded content and set cache limits.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Customizing Your Experience

Namida offers extensive customization options:

### Theme Settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Theme Settings">
    Go to **Settings** → **Theme**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Theme Mode">
    Select from:
    - Light
    - Dark  
    - Black (AMOLED)
    - System (auto)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable Dynamic Colors">
    Toggle **Auto Coloring** to have the player interface adapt to album artwork colors.
    
    <Note>
    Dynamic theming makes each song feel unique by extracting colors from the artwork!
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Essential Settings to Configure

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Playback Settings">
    **Settings → Playback**
    
    - **Crossfade Duration**: Smooth transitions between tracks (0-12 seconds)
    - **Replay Gain**: Normalize volume across different tracks
    - **Skip Silence**: Automatically skip silent parts
    - **Play Speed**: Adjust default playback speed
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Audio Quality">
    **Settings → Audio**
    
    - **YouTube Audio Quality**: Set preferred quality (high/medium/low)
    - **Data Saver Mode**: Reduce bandwidth usage
    - **Audio Only Mode**: Download only audio from YouTube videos
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="UI Customization">
    **Settings → Customization**
    
    - **Miniplayer Style**: Compact or card style
    - **Waveform**: Enable animated waveform on player
    - **Particles Effect**: Visual effects synchronized with music
    - **Grid Count**: Adjust album grid size
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Lyrics">
    **Settings → Lyrics**
    
    - **Auto-Fetch Lyrics**: Automatically download lyrics from online sources
    - **Synced Lyrics**: Show time-synced lyrics during playback
    - **Lyrics Source**: Choose preferred lyrics provider
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Smart Features to Try

### Sleep Timer

Set Namida to stop playing after a certain duration:

1. Open the player
2. Tap the ⋮ (more) menu
3. Select **Sleep Timer**
4. Choose duration or number of tracks

### Smart Track Generation

<Info>
Namida can generate intelligent playlists based on your listening habits!
</Info>

1. Open any track
2. Tap ⋮ (more) menu
3. Select **Add to Queue** → **Recommended Tracks**
4. Namida will add tracks you usually listen to around the same time

Other generation options:
- **Similar Release Date**: Tracks from the same era
- **Most Played**: Your top tracks from a specific period
- **Random**: Surprise me!

### History Tracking

Namida keeps detailed listening history:

1. Open navigation drawer
2. Tap **History**
3. Browse by day, week, or month
4. See listening statistics and patterns

<Note>
You can import your YouTube and Last.fm listening history to get complete statistics!
</Note>

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Music not showing up">
    1. Check **Settings → Indexer → Folders to Scan**
    2. Ensure folders contain supported audio files
    3. Tap **Re-scan** to force a fresh index
    4. Check file permissions on your music folder
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Lyrics not loading">
    1. Enable **Settings → Lyrics → Auto-Fetch Lyrics**
    2. Check internet connection
    3. Ensure track has proper metadata (title/artist)
    4. Try manually adding lyrics from the track menu
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="YouTube videos not playing">
    1. Check internet connection
    2. Update to latest Namida version
    3. Clear YouTube cache: **Settings → YouTube → Clear Cache**
    4. Try toggling **Settings → YouTube → Data Saver Mode**
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Battery drain issues">
    1. Disable **Settings → Customization → Particles Effect**
    2. Reduce **Settings → Customization → Waveform Intensity**
    3. Enable battery optimization for Namida in Android settings
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
